Mohammad El Smaily

About me

I am an Associate Professor in the Department of Mathematics & Statistics at the University of Northern British Columbia. I obtained my PhD in Mathematics from Aix-Marseille Université in 2008 under the supevision of François Hamel. After completing my PhD, I had three postdoctoral positions:

  • NSERC Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Toronto (St. George Capmus, 2011-2014).
  • Postdoctoral Associate at Carnegie Mellon University (Pittsburgh, 2010-2011).
  • PIMS Postdoctoral Fellowship at the University of British Columbia (Vancouver, 2008-2010).

Research interests

I am mainly interested in partial differential equations (PDE) and nonlinear analysis. Part of my work focuses on the qualitative properties of solutions to reaction-diffusion equations, which appear in studying population dynamics and mixing processes in heterogeneous frameworks. Keywords related to my work include: elliptic and parabolic non-linear PDE, traveling fronts and propagation phenomena, reaction-advection-diffusion systems, spectral theory, mathematical biology and ecology, population dynamics, integro-differential equations, and semilinear wave equations. For more details, see my publications page.
