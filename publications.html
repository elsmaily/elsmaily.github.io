Work in progress

I. Integro-difference equations

I am currently working on a nonlinear integral equation that appears in studying dispersal and growth of a population in patchy landscape. Mathematically, we have then a dicontinuous kernel in the equation as well as a nonlinearity. The model reads \[\begin{array}{l} \forall \,n\geq 1,~\forall x\in \Omega, \\ u_{n+1}(x)=\displaystyle{\int_\Omega k(x,y)F(u_n(y)){\rm d}y}, \end{array}\] where \(\Omega\) stands for the spatial domain (the habitat).

The value \(u_{n}(x)\) represents the population density in the \(n\)th year, at a location \(x\in\Omega.\) The dispersal kernel is the function \(k(x,y),\) which is assumed to be discontinuous at a finite number of points \(\{a_i\}_{1\leq i\leq m}\) in \(\Omega\), in order to reflect the patchy nature of the landscape.

We are studying the spectral properties of the linearized version of the above model. As well, we are looking for criteria, involving the principal eigevalue of the linearized operater, under which the model admits a nonzero equilibrium. This criterion is the one that gives the conditions under which the population persists and does not undergo extinction.
